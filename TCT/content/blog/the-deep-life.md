At Trinity Chapel Thika, we often say that our vision is to *Grow D.E.E.P in the Word*. This is not a catchy slogan — it is a framework that shapes how we structure our weeks, our small groups, and our personal spiritual lives.

## What Does D.E.E.P Mean?

**D — Daily Devotions.** The bedrock of any growing disciple is a personal, daily rhythm of scripture reading, prayer, and reflection. It does not need to be long. It does need to be consistent.

**E — eGroups.** Christianity was never designed to be solo. Our eGroups are small gatherings of 6–12 people who read the same passage together, ask honest questions, pray for each other, and hold each other accountable. These groups meet every Thursday.

**E — Engagement.** We are called to *do*, not just to know. Engagement means serving in a ministry, taking steps beyond your comfort zone, and letting faith produce visible action in your neighbourhood and family.

**P — Pulpit.** Every Sunday, TC Thika gathers around the preached Word. The Pulpit is where the community comes together to listen, respond, and be sent out again.

## Why Structure Matters

Growth rarely happens by accident. A tree that grows *deep* can withstand storms that would topple a shallow-rooted one. The DEEP framework is not a formula — it is an invitation to intentional, communal growth that is sustainable over a lifetime.

**Start where you are.** If you have no daily devotional practice, begin with five minutes and Psalm 23. If you are not in an eGroup, [reach out to us](/contact) and we will connect you. If you have never served, pick one ministry and give it three months.

Depth takes time. But it is absolutely worth it.
